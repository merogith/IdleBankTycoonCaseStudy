20 June 2026 Kolibri Games · Game Economy Designer

Dear Kolibri Games Team,

I take idle games apart for fun. I'll be a few prestiges deep in one and catch myself reverse-engineering the curve instead of playing it — where's the sink, why did this reward land now, what holds a player who already has everything? That instinct is why I'm writing about the Game Economy Designer role.

Rather than tell you I can do the work, I built it. I ran a full, self-directed economy-design study on Idle Bank Tycoon: a parameter-driven model (400+ live formulas) that surfaces the late-game problem — idle cash piling up to thousands of times the next upgrade's cost — and two live-ops levers to fix it: a hard-currency liquidity lock and income-scaled rewarded-ad tuning. Each is modelled end-to-end with ROI math, a sensitivity grid, retention guardrails and an A/B plan, landing blended ARPDAU +15.5% (~$2M/yr at static DAU). If I had to sequence them, I'd ship the ad tuning first and treat the loan as the bigger retention bet. The full study sits alongside this letter.

I got to economy design the long way round — three years turning messy data into decisions across pharma, edtech and equity research — but games are where the data and the design finally meet. I also build and ship my own browser games on engines I wrote from scratch, so a system reads to me from both the spreadsheet and the code. And idle is genuinely next on my own roadmap: an idle tycoon and a single-player idle RPG are the two projects I most want to build.

I'd love to bring that mix — analytical rigour, a designer's instinct, and a real love of the genre — to the team behind Idle Miner Tycoon. Thank you for reading; I'd welcome the chance to talk it through.

Warm regards,

Meriç Erler
